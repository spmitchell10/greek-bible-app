## Search Examples by Use Case

### For Theological Study
- **All occurrences of "love" (ἀγάπη)**: Lemma = `ἀγάπη`
- **"Believe" verbs**: Lemma = `πιστεύω`
- **Words for "grace"**: Lemma = `χάρις`

### For Grammar Study
- **Aorist passive indicative**: POS=verb, Tense=aorist, Voice=passive, Mood=indicative
- **Present participles**: POS=verb, Tense=present, Mood=participle
- **Genitive absolutes**: Case=genitive, Mood=participle

### For Syntax Study
- **Articular infinitives**: POS=verb, Mood=infinitive (then manually check for article)
- **Imperatives**: Mood=imperative
- **Vocatives**: Case=vocative

### For Vocabulary Building
- **All nouns in a book**: Book=Matthew, POS=noun
- **Frequent verb forms**: POS=verb (then analyze results)

## Tips for Effective Searching

1. **Lemma vs. Word Form**
   - Use lemma for all forms of a word
   - Use word search for specific inflected forms

2. **Combining Filters**
   - Start with one or two criteria
   - Add more filters to narrow results
   - Too many filters may return no results

3. **Understanding Context**
   - Always read the full verse
   - Check surrounding verses in your Bible
   - Consider the book's context

4. **Morphological Patterns**
   - Study patterns in specific books
   - Compare authors' writing styles
   - Track theological terms through NT
